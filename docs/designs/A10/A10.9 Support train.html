Support train

A neighbor-led care train tied to a verified address: meals after a baby, rides during chemo, dog walks after a surgery. The screen is shared between coordinators (who launched it) and contributors (who scroll, pick a slot, and follow through). It’s built around commitment density — at a glance, you can see how much help is still needed, where the gaps are, and what other neighbors are already bringing so you don’t double up the lasagna.

src/app/support-trains/[id].tsx

slot.header_pattern

recipient_card + type_dates_progress — the household is foregrounded (with a quote from them so the request feels human), then a green meal-train strip with dates, a 12 / 21 progress bar, and a stacked-avatar peek of who’s already on board.

slot.body_pattern

slot_calendar + slot_rows — 4-week grid uses dashed cells for Open, soft-green for Covered, sky for Today; legend pinned underneath. Below it, two row stacks: Open near you (each row has an inline "Sign up" pill) and Already on the train (showing who and what they’re bringing).

slot.primary_cta

sign_up_for_slot — sticky bottom bar with a soft fade into the page so the CTA never floats over content. In the covered state it splits into send_a_card + join_as_backup — the train doesn’t need another dinner but the recipient still deserves a note.

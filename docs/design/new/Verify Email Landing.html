Verify email — the deep-link landing

The screen the app opens when the user taps the link in their verification email. The A18 status archetype, collapsed — no timeline, because the token check is instantaneous: halo → headline → email-bolded body → status pill → CTA stack → shield-check footer. Three states for the three things that can happen on tap. Rendered in the shared beacon-family bezel.

Verify-first result. A18.1 verify-email-sent is the pre-tap “we sent you an email” state; A18.3 / A18.4 are timeline review waits. None of them is the post-tap landing — the loading, success, and expired states below did not exist. Built here as A18 collapsed-status variants on shared tokens.

The post-tap landing

Verifying → Verified → Expired had no screen. Added as three A18 status variants with the matching CTA contract.

Three states · what happens when the link is tapped
STATE 1 · VERIFYING
Checking the link
Mail halo wrapped in a spinning ring, neutral “Checking your link…” pill with its own spinner, no CTA — the transient moment while the token is validated.
STATE 2 · VERIFIED
“Email verified”
Green check halo, email confirmed in the body, success “Verified · just now” pill, and a single primary “Continue” with trailing arrow into the app.
STATE 3 · EXPIRED
“This link has expired”
Amber broken-link halo, 24-hour explanation, “Link expired” pill, primary “Resend verification”, and a ghost “Use a different email” fallback.
